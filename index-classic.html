Step 02 · The Recipient

Find your MP

Your postal code goes directly from your browser to Represent, a Canadian open-data service, to identify your riding. It is never sent to this site's operator, and nothing is stored. Prefer not to use it? Look your MP up on ourcommons.ca ↗ and paste their email into your mail app after copying the letter.

This bill is before the Senate. Senators are appointed to represent provinces and regions — so the letter goes to a senator from your province, not an MP.

Your province is worked out from the first letter of your postal code, right here in your browser — nothing is sent anywhere. A senator from your province is picked at random to spread letters fairly; switch to any other in the list. Directory: sencanada.ca ↗

Step 03 · The Letter

Send it in your own words

Edit freely — a letter in your own words carries more weight than a form letter. Personal stakes ("as a parent…", "as someone who works in…") matter most.

Why this works

MPs and senators weigh correspondence from the people they represent — it is tracked, tallied, and answered. A cited, specific letter cannot be dismissed as noise.

Every claim in this letter carries the section number of the bill it comes from, so the office can verify it in minutes.

The letter opens in your mail app and is sent from your address — a real constituent, not a platform. This page never sees it.

Recorded: nothing

This page has no accounts, no analytics, no cookies, no tracking pixels, and no database. It cannot see your name, your email, your postal code, or whether you sent anything. The one optional network request — the postal-code lookup — goes directly from your browser to Represent (Open North) and nowhere else. The letter is yours, sent by you, from your own email.
